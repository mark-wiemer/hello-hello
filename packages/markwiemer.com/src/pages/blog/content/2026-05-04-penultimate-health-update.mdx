Yeah, I'm working on it. Truly, I've appreciated all the support from family, loved ones, friends, coworkers, and acquaintances. Of course, the excellent doctors and medical staff I've worked with have also been helpful. This is arguably the sickest I've ever been:

- When I was 15-16, I collapsed many times in high school over the course of several months. The issue was likely teenage stress, not taking meds consistently, and heartburn from too much pasta.
- I've only had covid once: it started in December 2023 and turned into post-viral fatigue (arguably long covid) that lasted until early March 2024.
- Bronchitis on its own was rough when I got it for the first time in September 2025: it lasted until early November 2025.
- And now we're here.

This is the penultimate health update because I'm sick of blogging about this! That said, if you know me, feel free to reach out. I'm happy to text a bit with the latest news and whatnot, but I want to get back to blogging about AI, mental health, world news, and other things that aren't a big ol' pity party. The ultimate health update will come eventually and it will be titled "I'm finally healthy!" and it will be great. In the meantime, please trust that I am handling this with the help of my incredible support system.

And please don't think I've been writhing in pain at home for the past 50 days! I wrapped up work in my old role, [started my new role](/blog/got-dream-job), cooked the cacio e pepe pictured below, merged a [bugfix](https://github.com/dotnet/roslyn/pull/83205) and a [new feature](https://github.com/dotnet/roslyn/pull/83182), did a ton of [work on Mocha](https://github.com/mochajs/mocha/pulls?q=is%3Apr+is%3Aclosed+involves%3Amark-wiemer+closed%3A2026-03-15..2026-05-03), organized [my Luanti notes](https://github.com/mark-wiemer/hello-hello/commits/main/packages/luanti?since=2026-03-15&until=2026-05-03), [updated this website](https://github.com/mark-wiemer/hello-hello/commits/main/packages/markwiemer.com?since=2026-03-15&until=2026-05-03), played board games with friends, video games with more friends, and even went to Dave and Buster's with my little brother the evening before my ER visit! My brother won a jackpot, and I had a blast too! I am truly out here living a life, don't you fret, and I'll be back living life to the fullest soon enough. But this week, I rest.
